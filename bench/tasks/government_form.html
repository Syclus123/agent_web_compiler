Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Grant Program — Phase I Application

Application Deadline: September 15, 2025, 11:59 PM ET. Late submissions will not be accepted under any circumstances. Applications must be submitted through Grants.gov.

Program Overview

The Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) program is a highly competitive program that encourages domestic small businesses to engage in Federal Research/Research and Development (R/R&D) with the potential for commercialization. Through a competitive awards-based program, SBIR enables small businesses to explore their technological potential and provides the incentive to profit from its commercialization.

The Department of Commerce SBIR program focuses on research topics that advance the Department's mission areas, including telecommunications, weather forecasting, trade data analytics, cybersecurity standards, and manufacturing technology.

Phase I awards provide up to $200,000 over a 12-month period to establish the technical merit, feasibility, and commercial potential of the proposed R/D efforts.

Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible, applicants must meet ALL of the following criteria at the time of award:

  • Be a small business concern as defined by 13 CFR 121.702
  • Be organized for profit, with a place of business located in the United States
  • Be more than 50% directly owned and controlled by one or more individuals who are citizens of, or permanent resident aliens in, the United States
  • Have no more than 500 employees, including affiliates
  • The primary employment of the principal investigator must be with the small business at the time of award and during the conduct of the project
  • Have not received more than 15 Phase I awards in the preceding 5 fiscal years without receiving a Phase II award

Note: Nonprofit organizations, foreign-owned businesses, and businesses that are majority-owned by venture capital firms, hedge funds, or private equity firms are NOT eligible for SBIR funding.

Required Application Materials

A complete application package must include all of the following documents. Incomplete applications will be rejected without review.

  1. SF-424 (R&R) Application for Federal Assistance — The standard federal grant application form. Available at Grants.gov.
  2. Research Plan (15 pages maximum) — Technical objectives, work plan, related R/D, key personnel, and commercialization strategy.
  3. Budget and Budget Justification — Detailed cost breakdown using SF-424A form, plus narrative justification for each cost category.
  4. Company Commercialization Report (CCR) — Required for all applicants with prior SBIR/STTR awards. Generated through the SBA Company Registry at sbir.gov.
  5. Data Management Plan (2 pages maximum) — Describe how research data will be stored, shared, and preserved.
  6. Biographical Sketches — NSF-format biosketches for all key personnel (PI and co-PIs).
  7. Current and Pending Support — Disclosure of all current and pending financial support for the PI and co-PIs.
  8. Letters of Support (optional but recommended) — From potential customers, partners, or technical advisors.

Evaluation Criteria

Proposals will be evaluated by technical expert reviewers using the following criteria (listed in descending order of importance):

  1. Technical Merit and Innovation (40%) — Soundness of the research approach, degree of innovation, and awareness of the state of the art.
  2. Commercial Potential (30%) — Market opportunity, commercialization plan, and potential economic impact.
  3. Qualifications of Key Personnel (20%) — Relevant experience, publication record, and capability to perform the research.
  4. Adequacy of Facilities and Resources (10%) — Availability of equipment, facilities, and institutional support.

How to Apply

All applications must be submitted electronically through Grants.gov. Paper applications will not be accepted.

  1. Register your organization on SAM.gov (allow 2–4 weeks for processing)
  2. Register on Grants.gov as an Authorized Organization Representative
  3. Download the application package from Grants.gov (Opportunity Number: DOC-SBIR-2025-001)
  4. Complete all required forms and attachments
  5. Submit the complete application before the deadline

Legal Notices

This program is authorized under 15 U.S.C. § 638 and implemented in accordance with the SBA SBIR/STTR Policy Directive. The Department of Commerce reserves the right to negotiate the final terms and conditions of any award. All awards are subject to the availability of appropriated funds.

Applicants are advised that the Federal Government is under no obligation to make any award as a result of this solicitation. The Government may cancel this solicitation or any part of it at any time before an award is made.

Data submitted in the application may be subject to the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA). Applicants should mark proprietary information in accordance with the instructions in the full solicitation. For concerns regarding the handling of proprietary data, contact the SBIR Program Office at sbir@commerce.gov or call (202) 555-0199.
